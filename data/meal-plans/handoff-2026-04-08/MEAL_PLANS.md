## V5 — Muscle Building (CURRENT PRIMARY PLAN)

**Status:** ACTIVE. This is the primary rotation plan as of March 2026.

**Why V5 exists:** Matt shifted goals from pure weight loss to muscle building + cardiovascular endurance. Calories increased from 1,600-1,800 to 2,200 to support muscle protein synthesis while maintaining a ~700 kcal/day deficit for slow body recomposition (~1-1.5 lbs fat loss/week).

**Meals:**

### Breakfast (~500 kcal)
- 4 scrambled eggs cooked in 1 tbsp ghee
- 3 oz ground beef (90/10 lean)
- 1 oz pepper jack cheese
- **Seasoning:** smoked paprika, garlic powder, black pepper

### Lunch (~600 kcal)
- 8 oz chicken breast (baked at 375°F, 20-25 min to 165°F internal)
- 2 oz gouda cheese
- Side: 2 hard-boiled eggs with everything bagel seasoning
- **Seasoning:** onion powder, dried thyme, salt

### Post-Workout Snack (~250 kcal)
- 3 hard-boiled eggs
- 1 beef stick (Chomps brand)
- *Eat within 30 minutes of finishing workout*

### Dinner (~650 kcal)
- 8 oz chuck steak OR NY strip OR sirloin (rotate weekly)
- 1 tbsp herb butter (butter + rosemary + garlic)
- Side: 2 oz blue cheese crumbles
- **Seasoning:** coarse salt, cracked pepper, dried rosemary

### Evening Snack (~200 kcal)
- 1 oz macadamia nuts
- 1 cheese stick

**Daily Macros:** ~2,200 kcal | 165g protein | 145g fat | <12g carbs

**Key differences from V3/V4:**
- Calories: 1,600 → 2,200 (can't build muscle in massive deficit)
- Protein: 120g → 165g (~0.85g per lb bodyweight — optimal for MPS)
- Added post-workout snack (recovery window)
- 4 eggs at breakfast instead of 3
- 8 oz chicken at lunch instead of 6
- 8 oz steak at dinner instead of 5-6 oz
- New proteins: ground beef, chuck steak rotation
- Better cheese variety: pepper jack, gouda, blue cheese

## V6 — Lean Variety (Rotation Alternative)

**Status:** Used for week-to-week rotation with V5 to prevent monotony.

**Meals:**
- **Breakfast:** 3 eggs + 2 oz turkey sausage 93/7 lean + 1 oz sharp cheddar
  - Seasoning: oregano + garlic powder
- **Lunch:** 6 oz chicken breast + 2 oz havarti or provolone + 2 hard-boiled eggs
  - Seasoning: rosemary + black pepper
- **Dinner:** 6-8 oz sirloin steak + herb butter + feta crumbles
  - Seasoning: garlic powder + oregano + coarse salt
- **Snacks:** Beef sticks + macadamia nuts + cheese sticks

**Macros:** ~2,100-2,200 kcal | ~160g protein | ~140g fat | <12g carbs

**Key points:**
- Sirloin is the leanest steak cut — don't overcook (hot pan, 3-4 min/side, pull at 130-135°F for medium)
- Turkey sausage 93/7 instead of ground beef (leaner)
- Havarti/provolone for different cheese flavor
- Extra half tablespoon ghee when cooking to compensate for leaner meats

## APPROVED ADDITIONS & MODIFICATIONS

These were discussed and approved throughout conversations:

1. **Plain full-fat Greek yogurt** (Fage Total or Chobani Whole Milk Plain) — OK as post-work snack. 5-6g carbs per serving, 15-18g protein. Avoid flavored, low-fat, nonfat, or fruit-on-bottom varieties.

2. **Corned beef** — Keto-approved, high protein, zero carbs. Good for special occasions (St. Patrick's Day). Slow simmer with cabbage.

3. **Chuck pot roast** — Excellent for slow cooker prep. Buy on markdown at Safeway manager's special bin.

4. **Whipping cream** — Very keto-friendly. Good for coffee or cooking.

5. **Whole milk** — Borderline (12g carbs per cup). Use sparingly or for pastry baking only.

6. **Jimmy Dean breakfast sausage** — This is PORK. Matt bought it once. It does NOT fit the plan. Flagged.

## ROTATION STRATEGY

To prevent monotony, rotate between V5 and V6 weekly:

- **Week A:** V5 (ground beef breakfast, chicken lunch, chuck/NY strip dinner)
- **Week B:** V6 (turkey sausage breakfast, chicken lunch, sirloin dinner)
- **Week C:** V5 with steak variation (ribeye instead of chuck)
- **Week D:** V6 with protein variation (swap some chicken for turkey burgers)

The key principle: same macro targets, different proteins and cheese varieties each week.
